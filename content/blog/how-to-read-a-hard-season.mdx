Every so often a stretch of life goes heavy. Work stalls, a relationship strains, and the ground you were standing on turns out to belong to somebody else. When people arrive in that state, they usually want one thing from the chart: they want to know when it ends.

It is the fair question. It is also the wrong one, and the difference matters.

## Transits describe pressure, not punishment

A transit is a planet moving over a point in your birth chart. It marks a period when a particular part of your life is under pressure, being tested, or being asked to change shape.

The language people reach for is moral. They speak about being punished, or about the universe teaching them a lesson. Nothing in a chart supports that. The sky is not administering justice. A hard transit describes weather, and weather is indifferent. What it does is expose the parts of a structure that were already weak, which is why hard seasons so often break the thing you had privately known was not working.

## Timing is a window, not a date

Charts are good at seasons and bad at dates. A transit has a window, and inside that window there are usually two or three moments when it concentrates. That is genuinely useful: it tells you when to make the difficult call, and when to sit on your hands.

What it will not do is give you a day on which the difficulty ends and something better begins. Anybody promising that is not reading a chart, they are selling relief. Relief is easy to sell to somebody in a hard season, which is exactly why you should be careful who you buy it from.

## The question that actually helps

Instead of asking when it ends, ask what it is asking of you.

Hard seasons tend to be specific. They apply pressure to one thing: a boundary you have not held, a piece of work you have avoided, a truth you have been managing around instead of saying. Read the chart for the location of the pressure, and the demand usually becomes obvious. Often uncomfortably so.

That reframing is not a comfort, and it is not meant to be. It is the difference between a season that happens to you and a season you can do something inside of. Only one of those is survivable with your self-respect intact.

## If you are in one now

Be careful with your decisions and gentle with your evenings. Do not make an irreversible choice at the peak of a difficult transit if it can wait for the far side of it, because your reading of the situation is currently under load.
