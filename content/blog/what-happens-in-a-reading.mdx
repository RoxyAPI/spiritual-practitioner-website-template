People are often nervous before a first reading, and almost always for the same reason: they do not know what they are walking into. Here is the whole shape of it, so you can decide whether it is for you before you spend anything.

## Before we meet

You send three things: your birth date, your birth time, and your birth city. That is all the chart needs.

I read the chart before the session, not during it. That matters. You are not paying to watch someone calculate; you are paying for the hour after the work is done, when there is something to talk about.

If you have a specific question, send it. If all you have is a vague sense that something is stuck, send that instead. Vague is a perfectly good place to start, and naming the vagueness is usually half the session.

## The hour itself

We meet on video. I start with what the chart is built out of, in plain language, and then we go to the thing you actually came with.

You can interrupt. You can disagree. If something I say does not land, say so, because a reading that you nod along to and forget by Thursday was a waste of your money. The useful ones are the ones you argue with.

Somewhere in the middle, most sessions turn. The question you booked with turns out to be standing in front of a different question, and that second one is the real one. That is normal. It is most of why people come back.

You get the recording afterwards. Nobody remembers a reading properly while they are inside it.

## What you are allowed to ask

Anything about the pattern you are in. What this season tends to ask of people. Why a particular relationship keeps snagging on the same thing. Whether the thing you are afraid of is actually the thing to be afraid of. Where your own chart says you tend to overreach, and where you tend to undersell yourself.

## What a reading is the wrong tool for

Medical questions. Legal questions. Financial advice. If a question needs a doctor, a lawyer, or an accountant, that is who it needs, and I will tell you so rather than guess.

Predictions of a fixed future, too. A chart describes conditions, not outcomes. Anyone who tells you they can name the month you will meet someone is selling certainty, which is the one thing this practice cannot honestly supply.

## Afterwards

Most people are quiet for a day or two, then they write. The reading is not the end of anything. It is a set of better words for the thing you were already living through, and words are what make a decision possible.
